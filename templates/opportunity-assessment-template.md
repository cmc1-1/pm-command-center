## Formatting Guidelines

- **Be rigorous with the scoring matrix:** The value of this template is in forcing honest assessment. Score generously and you defeat the purpose. If you don't have customer evidence, score it low and state what you need to learn.
- **Separate opinion from evidence:** In every section, distinguish between what you know (data, research, customer quotes) and what you believe (hypotheses, intuition). Both are valuable, but readers need to know which is which.
- **The validation plan is not optional:** Even a "strong pursue" should have a validation plan. The question is not IF to validate but WHAT to validate first.
- **One paragraph, one opportunity:** The summary should describe exactly one opportunity. If you find yourself describing two things, split them into separate assessments.
- **Revisit parked opportunities:** Set a calendar reminder. The best opportunities are often ones whose timing was wrong the first time around.
- **Keep scoring consistent across assessments:** If your team evaluates multiple opportunities, calibrate scoring so a "4" means the same thing every time. Document your calibration norms.
- **Target length:** 3-5 pages. Detailed enough to make a sound decision, concise enough to be read in one sitting.
