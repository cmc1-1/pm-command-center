## Formatting Guidelines

- **Executive Summary:** Write this last. It should stand alone — a reader who only reads this section should understand the core proposal and expected outcome.
- **Quantify everything:** Replace adjectives like "significant" or "many" with specific numbers. If you don't have exact numbers, provide ranges or estimates with stated assumptions.
- **Be honest about risks:** Leadership trusts business cases that acknowledge uncertainty more than ones that pretend everything is guaranteed.
- **Alternatives section:** Always include "do nothing" as a baseline. Show that you considered multiple paths, not just your preferred one.
- **Keep it concise:** Target 3-5 pages for the core content. Move detailed analysis to the appendix.
- **Use the tables:** They force precision and make it easy for reviewers to compare options and assess completeness.
- **State assumptions explicitly:** If your ROI depends on assumptions (adoption rate, market growth, etc.), call them out so reviewers can challenge them.
