## Formatting Guidelines

- **Lead with outcomes, not activities:** "Increased conversion by 25%" is stronger than "Ran A/B tests on the onboarding flow." Start every bullet with impact, then explain how.
- **Use "I" not "we":** This document is about your contribution. Acknowledge the team's role, but be precise about what you personally did. Reviewers need to distinguish your impact from the team's.
- **Quantify relentlessly:** Numbers are more credible than adjectives. Replace "significantly improved" with "improved by 35%." If exact numbers aren't available, use reasonable estimates and note they are estimates.
- **Before/after is powerful:** The results table forces you to show the delta. A metric without a baseline is hard to evaluate.
- **Collect testimonials proactively:** Don't wait until review season. After a successful project, ask 2-3 stakeholders for a sentence or two about your contribution while it's fresh.
- **Be honest in lessons learned:** Genuine reflection is more impressive than perfection. Showing what you learned signals growth potential.
- **Match competencies to your level:** For promotion packets, map your actions to the competencies expected at the NEXT level, not your current one. Show you are already operating there.
- **One narrative per achievement:** If you have multiple accomplishments to document, create separate narratives for each. Bundle them in a single document only if they are closely related.
