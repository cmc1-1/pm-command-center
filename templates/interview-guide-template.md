### Interviewer mindset
- You are learning, not validating. Be genuinely curious.
- Silence is a tool. After they answer, wait 3 seconds — they often elaborate.
- Follow the energy. If they get animated about something, go deeper even if it's off-script.
- Never lead. "Tell me about..." not "Don't you think..."
- Capture behavior (what they do), not opinion (what they say they'd do).

## Interview Script

### Opening (3-5 minutes)
Purpose: Build rapport, set expectations, get permission.

> "Thanks for taking the time. I'm [name], a product manager at [company]. I'm trying to understand how people like you [handle X / think about Y] — there are no right or wrong answers, and I'm genuinely here to learn from you. I might ask some questions that feel obvious — that's intentional because I want to hear it in your words rather than assume. This will take about [duration] and with your permission I'll record so I can focus on our conversation instead of notes. Is that OK? Do you have any questions before we start?"

### Warm-up: Context & role (5 minutes)
Purpose: Understand who they are and what their world looks like.

- "Tell me about your role — what does a typical week look like for you?"
- "How does [the problem area] fit into your day-to-day responsibilities?"
- "Who else in your organization touches [this area]?"

*Listen for: seniority signals, decision-making authority, team structure, how much they personally deal with the problem vs. delegate it.*

### Core section 1: Current state & JTBD (10-15 minutes)
Purpose: Understand the job they're trying to get done and how they do it today.

**Trigger & context:**
- "Think about the last time you had to [do the thing we're investigating]. Walk me through what happened — start from the very beginning."
- "What triggered that? What made you realize you needed to do it?"

**Current workflow (step by step):**
- "And then what did you do? And after that?"
- "What tools or resources did you use?"
- "How long did the whole process take?"
- "Who else was involved?"

**Pain points (let them surface naturally):**
- "What was the hardest part of that?"
- "Where did things slow down or go wrong?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about that process, what would it be?"

**Workarounds:**
- "Have you found any tricks or shortcuts to make this easier?"
- "What do you wish you had that doesn't exist?"

*Listen for: emotional language (frustrated, annoyed, love, hate), time sinks, manual steps that should be automated, handoff problems between people or tools.*

### Core section 2: Deeper exploration (10-15 minutes)
Purpose: Probe specific areas relevant to the research question.

**[Custom questions based on research focus — replace these]:**
- "[Question specific to your hypothesis 1]"
- "[Question specific to your hypothesis 2]"
- "[Question about a specific feature or approach you're considering — but frame it as exploration, not validation]"

**Probing techniques (use throughout):**
- "Tell me more about that."
- "What do you mean by [term they used]?"
- "Why is that important to you?"
- "Can you give me a specific example?"
- "What happened next?"
- "How did that make you feel?" (use sparingly — but emotional data is valuable)
- "You mentioned [X] — how does that connect to [Y]?"

**The 5 Whys (for root cause):**
When they state a problem, ask "why is that a problem?" up to 5 times to reach the root cause. Stop when you hit a fundamental human need (save time, reduce risk, look competent, avoid effort).

### Core section 3: Outcomes & value (5-10 minutes)
Purpose: Understand what success looks like and what they'd pay for.

- "If this process worked perfectly, what would be different for you?"
- "How would you measure whether a solution is actually working?"
- "What would you be able to do that you can't do today?"
- "Have you looked at any other solutions? What did you think of them?"
- "If a solution existed that [brief description], how would that change things for you?"

*Listen for: willingness to pay signals, switching triggers, what "good enough" vs. "great" looks like, competitive products they've tried.*

### Closing (3-5 minutes)
Purpose: Capture anything missed, leave the door open.

- "Is there anything about [topic] that I should have asked but didn't?"
- "Anything else you want to share?"
- "Would it be OK if I follow up with a quick question or two later?"
- "Do you know anyone else who deals with this challenge who might be willing to chat?"

> "This has been incredibly helpful — thank you. We'll use these insights to build something better, and I'd love to share what we come up with when it's ready."

## Anti-patterns to Avoid

| Don't | Do Instead |
|-------|-----------|
| "Would you use a feature that does X?" | "How do you handle X today?" |
| "Don't you think X is a problem?" | "Tell me about your experience with X" |
| "Our product can do X — what do you think?" | "What would your ideal solution look like?" |
| "On a scale of 1-10, how important is X?" | "Walk me through the last time X affected your work" |
| Ask about future behavior ("Would you...") | Ask about past behavior ("Last time you...") |
| Ask compound questions | One question at a time, then probe |
| Fill silence | Wait 3-5 seconds after they finish — they'll often go deeper |
| Take notes frantically | Record the session, be present, jot only key words |
