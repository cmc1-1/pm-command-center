## Formatting Guidelines

- **Lead with narrative, not features:** This document tells the story of your strategy. Feature lists belong in backlogs and PRDs. If someone reads only the first three sections, they should understand your direction.
- **Themes over timelines:** Organize by strategic intent, not by quarter. Time horizons (Now/Next/Later) provide sequencing without false precision.
- **Be honest about confidence levels:** "Now" should be high confidence. "Later" is directional. Do not pretend you know the exact scope of work 9 months from now.
- **Name trade-offs explicitly:** What you are NOT doing is just as strategic as what you are doing. This section prevents scope creep and manages expectations.
- **Keep it fresh:** Update this document at least quarterly. Stale roadmap narratives erode trust. Mark the "Last Updated" date prominently.
- **Open questions are a strength:** Listing what you don't know signals intellectual honesty and invites collaboration. Update this section as questions get resolved.
- **Target length:** 4-6 pages. Enough depth to be useful, short enough to actually get read.
